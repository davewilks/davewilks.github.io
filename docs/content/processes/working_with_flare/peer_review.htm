Flare Peer Review Process

Summary:

To review a help topic in Flare.

Full Description:

Before you submit your completed Flare topics for external review, conduct an internal peer review with a TCOMM writer. While the SME/functional reviews focus on functionality and content, the peer review is editorial in nature, may have a walkthrough of the interface if available, and ensures that your work maintains the standards of the TCOMM department.

To submit your work for review:

  1. In Flare, check in the topics that you want reviewed. Your reviewer must check these out in order to track changes and contribute suggestions.
  2. Use the peer review process in the JIRA ticket to put the ticket into the general review bucket or assign to a specific writer.

To review another author's work:

  1. In Flare, check out the latest help files that you are going to review.
  2. Lock the topics until your review is complete. Do not leave topics checked out for long periods of time. Check the topics back in with comments in the file, in the check-in history, or in the JIRA ticket so the author knows your progress.
  3. Click Review > Track Changes.
  4. Edit the page for grammar, punctuation, voice, and stylistic consistency. Track changes and use annotation for comments to the author.
    1. To edit the content of a snippet, check out its source file.
    2. Make your edits in the snippet source file, and then click Save.
    3. In the topic that contains the snippet, insert an annotation about the snippet file edit.
  5. Conduct a UI walkthrough if the product is available.
  6. Save your edits, and then check in the topics.
  7. The author may choose to review the edits with you.
  8. The author can verify all changes using Review > Show Changes.
