Peer Reviewer

Summary:

The following steps define how to add a peer reviewer to a JIRA ticket.

Full Description:

The cycle for reviewing TCOMM help topics includes a peer review from another writer. Adding a peer review to a JIRA ticket is straightforward for all TCOMM projects.

NOTE If you are working on a ticket that is an update, hotfix, or maintenance to a topic in production from a previous release, you may decide to forego the peer review step as it may be unnecessary. If you choose to skip the peer review, enter your name in the Peer Approver field in the JIRA ticket to move through the workflow without engaging a peer reviewer. Also add a comment to the ticket as to why the peer review isn't necessary.

As a reviewer, tickets are either assigned to you or you can begin the peer review process by assigning yourself a ticket from the Help Content Ready for Peer Review bucket. T

CompConnect has, generally, only one review cycle. If there are items to resolve in that review, the writers use IM or Lync with the SME/functional reviewer. For WAPs, the JIRA ticketing process is the same but the review is in the tutorial/walkthrough, not the Flare topic.

Solution:

To complete a peer review:

  1. Edit the content in the source.
  2. After you complete the review, enter your name in the Peer Approver field and assign the ticket back to the Documentation Lead or previously assigned writer, depending on who is actually working on the ticket.

    3. If you'd like to see it again, assign it back to the writer without your name in the Peer Approver field.

  3. Enter the time that you spent in the JIRA ticket in the Time Tracking fields.
