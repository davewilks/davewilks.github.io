Working on JIRA Tickets

Summary:

Describes the workflow of JIRA tickets for TCOMM writers.

Full Description:

This process describes how to manage tickets assigned to you and send them for peer review.

Solution:

Send to Development

While developing content for your topic, move it to the Development In Progress status. To do this, move it from triage to Development In Progress.

  1. Assign the ticket and get it into the Development in Progress status:
    1. Click Send to Development.

    2. In the Assignee box, enter the name of the writer.
    3. In the Documentation Lead box, enter the name of the writer.
    4. Click Send to Development.

      5. The ticket appears on My Help Content in Development or Triage widget.

  2. Update the Description field - Enter any information you want to pass on to reviewers or other writers here.

Send to Peer Review

To ensure cleear and consistent content, your content will be reviewed by your peers. When you are ready to have your content peer reviewed, complete these steps:

  1. Check in all of the content that you want to have reviewed, including snippets.
  2. In the JIRA ticket, click Request Peer Review.

  3. Change the Assignee:
    1. to Unassigned to put it in the review bucket.

      2. The ticket will appear in the Help Content Ready for Peer Review and My Help Content in Peer Review gadgets.

    2. a specific reviewer if you know the peer reviewer.

      3. The ticket will appear in the reviewer's My Help Content to Peer Review and the writer's My Help Content in Peer Review gadgets.

  4. In the Time Spent box, enter the time you spent developing the content that hasn't already been accounted for.
  5. Click Request Peer Review.

Receive from Peer Review and Send to Functional Review

After your peer approves your content, they will assign it back to you. Make the changes and feel free to have a conversation with your reviewer. After you implement the changes, move it to Functional Review so your SME reviewers can review it for accuracy.

NOTE This process might be different on a project by project basis. It might also differ based on whether it is backlog content or content that supports an agile team.

  1. Click Request Functional Review.
  2. In the Assignee box, enter the name of your reviewer.
    1. CompConnect - Assign it to yourself. Reviews are handled in the scrum process.
    2. TRSS backlog topic- Assign it to Lauri Harrison, who will route the review to the appropriate reviewer.
    3. TRSS sprint-related topic - Assign it to yourself. Reviews are handled in the scrum process.
  3. Attach a copy of the topic to review (if applicable).
  4. Add comments to help the reviewer.
  5. Click Request Functional Review.
