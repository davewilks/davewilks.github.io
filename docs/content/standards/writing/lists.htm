Lists

Summary:

Describes the usage of numbered and bulleted lists, and writing and punctuation in lists that have stem sentences.

Full Description:

Punctuation and Writing

  • Introduce a list with a sentence or fragment stem ending with a colon, regardless of whether it is ordered or not ordered.
  • Begin each entry in a list with a capital letter.
  • Make entries in a list parallel in writing structure.
  • End each entry with a period if all entries are complete sentences, a mixture of fragments and sentences, or they all complete the introductory sentence or fragment.
  • Exception: When all entries are short imperative sentences (three words or fewer) or single words. These entries do not need ending punctuation. If all entries are fragments that together with the introductory phrase do not form a complete sentence, and do not end them with periods.
  • If you introduce a list with a fragment, do not treat the list and its introduction like one continuous sentence.
  • Do not use semicolons or commas to end list items.
  • Do not write 'and' before the last list item.

Examples

  • If all entries are a mixture of fragments and sentences.
  • If all entries complete the introductory sentence or fragment.

Ordered Lists (Procedures)

For procedures or other sequential lists:

  • Introduce the procedure with an infinitive statement that ends with a colon.
  • Use numbers to set off the list.
  • Use at least two numbered steps in the list.

Example

  1. Click Pay Types.
  2. Click Add Pay Type.
  3. Click the Pay Type list, and then select a standard pay type.
  4. In the Display As box, enter the name of the pay type.
  5. Click Save.
