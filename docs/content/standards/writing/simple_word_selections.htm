Simple Word Choices

Full Description:

Use simple words in your writing to make the text clearer and more translatable. See Readability and Translation Issues.

Use:

  • 'Begin' not Commence
  • 'In' not within
  • 'Open' or 'Start', not Launch
  • 'To' not In order to
  • 'Hover over' not position mouse or move the mouse over...

In vs. Within

In most technical writing, we use the preposition 'in' instead of 'within'.

  • In denotes inclusion, location, position, something that is a part of something else, and into. http://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/in
  • Within means enclosure, containment, and in the limits or boundary of something. http://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/within?show=0&t=1404834972

Example: In the Name field, enter the applicant's name.

Example: Approve the feedback within two days or the application archives the review.
