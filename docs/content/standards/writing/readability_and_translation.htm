Voice

Summary:

Towers Watson is a storied company with a long history. We are a trusted advisor and also an expert in the fundamental businesses behind the software products the Tech Comm team supports.

Who we are informs the voice of our content. The voice represents the company and does not change. However, our tone can change based on deliverable (a whitepaper takes a more marketing tone than a help topic, which has a more no nonsense tone). With that in mind, follow these voice guidelines:

  1. We use the voice of a trusted advisor. We speak in a direct, authorative manner. We get it right and are reliable. We are not rude, but we do use imperative, unambiguous sentences.
  2. We are business casual. We don't have a problem using contractions, but we aren't cheeky or overly casual either.
  3. We are succinct. We describe things clearly and don't mince words. We use active, short sentences to ensure clarity.
  4. We are global. We avoid idioms and culturally sensitive terminology. We recognize our customers are from all around the world and treat each of them with respect.

Solution:

  1. Avoid long sentences. See sentence length.
  2. Use active voice.
  3. Avoid jargon or culturally-specific terms.
  4. Avoid ambiguity.
  5. Use the relative pronoun that after verbs. See That.
    For example ensure that, or verify that. See the Content Rules blog for some good reasons why.
  6. Use consistent terminology. A glossary is a must. Describe the same thing the same way.
  7. Avoid idioms.
  8. Avoid Latin terms such as i.e., e.g., etc.
  9. Do use articles, pronouns, and linking verbs to write more succinctly.
